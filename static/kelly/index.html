Travel Information

Flying Into Denver

The closest major airport is Denver International Airport (DIA), a 3 hour drive from Salida. DIA is a United/Frontier hub with direct flights from most major cities. We recommend renting a car at the airport, or arranging a carpool with other guests (we'll connect carpoolers after the RSVP deadline).

Driving 3h to Salida

The drive takes about 2.5h from Denver and 3h from DIA/Boulder (plan buffer time for traffic on Friday/Sunday evenings). The direct route, US-285-S, is highly scenic. If you have an extra hour or more, detour over Guanella Pass, a spectacular drive above treeline with plentiful hike and photo stops.

Lodging

We have reserved the entire property for the weekend, and recommend you allow us to arrange your rooms.


More information is available on the Blue Coyote Ranch website. Detailed options are presented in the RSVP form.

Local Activities

  • Check out nearby Salida, a charming mountain town with good restaurants, waterfront views, and art galleries.
  • Hike the half mile trail around the ranch, or up a 14er (Mt. Shavano).
  • Raft the Arkansas River through town.
  • Backcountry ski at Monarch Pass (snow permitting).
  • Day trip to Great Sand Dunes National Park, Kelly's favorite sand and water playground.
  • Relax at Valley View Hot Springs (a magical place with naked hippies) or Mt. Princeton Resort (a more traditional hot spring spa).

Weather

June weather in Salida is typically sunny during the day (70-85°F) and cool in the evenings (45-55°F). Brief afternoon thunderstorms are common, and the sun at high elevation is intense. Bring layers, sunglasses, and a sun hat.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I wear to the wedding?
Cocktail/garden party attire. The ceremony will be outdoors, so we recommend practical shoes suitable for grass and gravel paths, and a light jacket.
Is there parking available at Blue Coyote Ranch?
Yes, Blue Coyote Ranch has ample on-site parking for all guests.
Are children welcome?
Children are enthusiastically welcomed.
Are pets welcome?
Dogs are warmly welcomed, though unfortunately the ranch is not suitable for other pets. The Blue Coyote has an off-leash area where Wish had a great time chasing rabbits. Overnight guests will be asked to cover a $25/day pet fee.
Will the ceremony be indoors or outdoors?
The ceremony will be held outdoors at Blue Coyote Ranch with a beautiful mountain backdrop, weather permitting. In case of inclement weather, we have a reception tent, and nearby indoor gathering space in the lodge.
Will I get altitude sick?
Salida sits at 7,000 feet elevation. In our experience, the large majority of visitors can ascend to this directly from sea level without trouble. Sensitive individuals may wish to arrive in Denver a day early to acclimate, or take Diamox.
What if a 3h drive into the Colorado mountains is not my thing?
We love the Colorado mountains and love to spend time in them with friends and family, but we understand that a mountain retreat 3h from Denver is not the right fit for everyone or every season of life. Please do not undertake travel you won't enjoy. We can find other ways to honor our connection.
