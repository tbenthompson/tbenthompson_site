Travel Information

Flying Into Denver

Denver International Airport (DIA) is the nearest major airport, with direct flights from most major cities. We recommend renting a car at the airport, or arranging a carpool with other guests (we'll connect carpoolers near the date).

Driving 3h to Salida

The drive takes about 2.5h from Denver and 3h from DIA/Boulder (plus traffic on Friday/Sunday evenings). The direct route, US-285-S, is super scenic.

If you have an extra hour, take a detour over Guanella Pass: a spectacular road that goes high above treeline with great hike and photo stops.

Lodging

The Blue Coyote can accommodate most guests on-site in a mix of lodge rooms, luxury cabins, and RV sites. More details are available at Blue Coyote Ranch website. Rooms and payment will be arranged through the venue closer to the date; early interest can be indicated in the RSVP form.

There are other hotels nearby in Salida, 5-10 minutes drive away.

Our Story

Parallel paths

2010-2023

We first met on a MITOC hike in 2012 (we think)! We both majored in Geology at MIT, and did separate field projects under the same professor (Ben in Mongolia, Kelly in India). After graduation, we both did PhDs (Ben modeled earthquakes, Kelly studied snow) and ran into each other at the yearly conference for the fellowship program that funded both of us. We spent weekends in the mountains - Ben running the trails in the White Mountains, Kelly skiing and hiking in Colorado. Our relationship began to grow after Ben moved to Colorado in 2023.

First Date

December 10, 2023

After a few weeks of back-and-forth (aka, strategic pursuit by Ben) we had our first date at Copper Mountain, Colorado. Skiing turned into kissing in the woods, then dinner, then an endless flow of laughter and conversation that hasn't stopped.

Moved In Together

October 15, 2024

We moved in together on an extended work trip to SF in Fall 2023. Life was unexpectedly hard - Ben started an intense new job, Kelly got pneumonia, and Wish Dogg rejected the city in favor of a strange shower fixation - but our relationship stayed easy. Two months later we moved into our current home in Boulder, Colorado, where we have been delightfully happy.

The Proposal

March 15, 2025

Kelly told Ben to show up at 2pm for a mystery date. There were small cards hidden throughout the nearby mountains. Each card had a clue to find the next card and several of the cards said that Ben would get style points for taking a straight line route to the next card. Kelly giggled in the background while Ben hunted for them. One of the last cards suggested looking at their GPS track and it was a heart. When they got back to the car, Kelly had hidden a bunch of nice clothes in the trunk. She told him they were going to walk a half mile or so to a nearby restaurant. On the walk there, Ben found a vase of flowers on the side of the trail with a final, 20th card that said "Will you marry me?"

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I wear to the wedding?
Cocktail/formal garden party attire - wear something that makes you feel beautiful! The ceremony will be outdoors, so your comfort may be improved by sunglasses, a sun hat, a light jacket, and shoes suitable for grass and gravel.
Are children welcome?
Children are enthusiastically welcomed.
Are pets welcome?
Dogs are warmly welcomed, and the Blue Coyote has an off-leash area where Wish had a great time chasing rabbits. Overnight guests will be asked to cover a $25/day dog fee.

Unfortunately, the ranch is not suitable for other pets.
What is the weather like?
June in Salida is typically warm and sunny during the day (70-85°F) and cool in the evenings (45-55°F). Brief afternoon thunderstorms are common, and the high-elevation sun is intense. Bring layers, sunglasses, and a sun hat. Hikers will likely find snow at higher elevations.
Will I get altitude sick?
Salida's elevation (7,000 ft) is safely below the threshold where altitude symptoms typically begin. Sensitive individuals may experience slight symptoms such as headaches and fatigue, which can be relieved with over-the-counter painkillers. It's very common to feel mild altitude sickness during strenuous activity at higher elevations (e.g., hiking at a mountain pass on the drive over). Simply descend back to lower elevations to feel better.
Can I park at the venue?
Yes, Blue Coyote Ranch has on-site parking for all guests.
What if a 3h drive into the Colorado mountains is not my thing?
That's OK! We love the Colorado mountains and love to spend time in them with friends and family, but we understand that a mountain retreat 3h from Denver is not the right fit for everyone or every season of life. Please don't do something you won't enjoy. We can find other ways to honor our connection.
